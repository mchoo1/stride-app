1.Executive Summary

Stride is a one-stop health app for Singapore — making it effortless to know what to eat, where to move, and whether you're tracking toward your goal. The April 2026 v2.0 spec strips the experience to four pillars users actually open the app for: Track, Monitor, Eat Smart, Move Nearby.

Why now

Global calorie-tracking apps (MyFitnessPal, Lose It!, Cronometer) are built around US/UK packaged food databases. They poorly index the Singaporean food reality — hawker dishes, NTUC FairPrice ready meals, 7-Eleven onigiri, GrabFood combos, café bowls. Stride owns this gap with a verified SG database, AI photo recognition, and Google Places-powered "what to eat / where to move" recommendations tied to remaining calorie budget.

The ask (this plan)

  • Lock the v2.0 simplified spec, complete the MVP build, and ship a TestFlight + Play closed beta within ~10 weeks.
  • Run a Singapore-only public launch in Q3 2026 with a goal of 5,000 MAU by end of Q4 2026.
  • Sign 3–5 anchor partners (1 wellness clinic, 1 gym chain, 1 healthier-eating restaurant group, 1 supermarket, 1 corporate wellness pilot) by end of Phase 4.
  • Activate freemium revenue (Stride+ at S$6.90/month) once the database and Eat/Move recommendations are stable.

2.Problem & Opportunity

The user problem

  • Tracking is tedious. Calorie apps require typing food names that don't return Singaporean results, then guessing portions.
  • Decisions are unguided. Users know they have "calories left" but don't know what to actually order at lunch — and global apps can't suggest a NTUC ready-meal or a Toast Box wrap.
  • Activity is disconnected. Move data sits in Apple Health / Fitbit but isn't tied back to "where can I burn the gap nearby?"
  • Body progress is opaque. Users don't have a clean BMR/TDEE baseline updated as their weight changes.

The market opportunity

Singapore residents are among the most health-conscious in SEA, with high smartphone penetration, mature wearable adoption (Apple Watch, Garmin, Fitbit) and government tailwinds (HPB's National Steps Challenge, Healthier Choice Symbol, Healthier SG). Yet there is no leading SG-native health-tracking app — global incumbents dominate by default, with weak local fit.

Insight: The MyFitnessPal experience in Singapore is "search bowl → 7 wrong results → guess." Stride's wedge is making the SG-specific answer the first answer.

4.Product Overview

v2.0 spec (April 2026): four pillars, five-tab navigation, AI-first logging, Google Places-powered "what / where" recommendations.

Pillar 1

Track

Calories in vs. out, AI photo scan, manual log, wearable sync — answers "how am I doing today?"

Pillar 2

Monitor

Weight, BF%, BMI, BMR, TDEE — visible baselines and a trend line.

Pillar 3

Eat Smart

Restaurant + ready-to-eat suggestions tailored to remaining calorie budget.

Pillar 4

Move Nearby

Map of nearby gyms, parks, trails, studios with distance and hours.

What we are explicitly NOT shipping in v2.0

The original brief envisioned community feed, workout sharing, professional booking and a service-provider portal as Phase 2/3. The v2.0 simplification correctly defers all of these to maintain focus. They re-enter as Phase 3 of this plan (Marketplace), only after retention is proven on the 4 core pillars.

Data & AI assets

  • SG Convenience & Grocery Nutrition Database (v3): 352 verified SKUs across 7-Eleven and NTUC FairPrice (cooked meals + groceries) with calories, protein, carbs, fat, source URL. This is the seed of the local food moat.
  • AI Photo Scan: Vision model returns dish guess + macros, cross-referenced with the SG DB for confirmation.
  • Google Places API: Powers Eat (restaurants) and Move (gyms/parks/trails) screens with distance + hours.
  • Wearable APIs: Apple HealthKit + Fitbit Web API at MVP; Garmin + Google Fit at Phase 2.

5.Strategy & Differentiation

Strategic principles

  1. Singapore-native, not Singapore-translated. Build for hawker, kopitiam, NTUC, 7-Eleven, GrabFood realities first. Every other tracker in the market treats SG as a long-tail locale.
  2. Decision over data. Most apps tell you "you ate X calories." Stride tells you "you have 620 kcal left — try the FairPrice high-protein wrap (S$5.90, 350 kcal, 28g protein) 2 mins away."
  3. Effortless logging beats complete logging. Photo scan + verified SG DB + recent foods strip should make logging a 3-second action, not a 30-second search.
  4. Earn community before building it. Don't ship a social tab on day one. Earn user trust on tracking, then introduce social Favorites and creator content (per ideas.md) once retention is proven.
  5. Partnerships compound. Each onboarded restaurant or gym both adds value and reduces user friction — this becomes the Phase 4 wedge.

6.Positioning & Brand

Positioning Statement

For health-aware Singaporeans who eat out and want to stay on track, Stride is the simplest one-stop health app that turns your daily calorie budget into clear answers — what to eat nearby, where to move, and how your body is trending. Unlike global trackers built on US food data, Stride is built on a verified Singapore food database and Google Places, so the first answer is always the right answer for here.

Brand Promise

Move. Eat. Connect. Stride makes the healthy choice the easy choice — in your neighbourhood, in your language, in 3 seconds.

Tone & voice

  • Clear, friendly, not preachy. Talks like a knowledgeable friend, not a coach.
  • Plain English (e.g. "Your body burns ~1,680 kcal just existing.") — not gym jargon.
  • Visually clean, generous whitespace, single hero numbers, calm green accent for "on track."

Naming & tagline candidates

  • Primary: Stride — Move. Eat. Connect.
  • Alt 1: Stride. Singapore's simplest health companion.
  • Alt 2: Eat smart. Move nearby. Stride.

Phase 1 — Product Launch (Q2 2026)

Goal: ship a focused, beautiful MVP and get it in real users' hands.

  • Build: Home (net cal hero), Scan (Vision AI), Eat (Google Places list), Move (Places map + activity logger), Me (weight/BF%/BMR/TDEE).
  • Strip: water, streak, community, workouts library — per v2.0 spec.
  • Data: Seed DB with the 352-SKU SG nutrition file; structure for community-verified additions later.
  • QA: Internal dogfooding 2 weeks → closed TestFlight beta 150 invitees → bug bash → soft launch.
  • Go/no-go gate: ≥70% of beta testers reach Day-3 retention before public launch.

Phase 2 — App Enhancement & Testing (Q3 2026)

Goal: harden the experience with the data and signals from public launch.

  • AI scan tuning: Targeted retraining on SG dishes (kaya toast, chicken rice, kway teow, FairPrice ready meals). Target 80% top-3 accuracy.
  • Wearables: Add Garmin Connect IQ + Google Fit; resolve duplicate-step issues on dual-device users.
  • Database expansion: Grow to 1,000 SKUs by adding Cold Storage, Cheers, FairPrice Xtra dishes; stand up a lightweight community-add flow with confidence scoring.
  • Reliability: Crash-free sessions ≥99.5%; cold-start <1.5s; energy/battery audit.
  • Body monitoring: Smart-scale integration (Withings, Fitbit Aria); BF% trend chart; weekly PDF export.
  • Go/no-go gate: D30 retention ≥20% on the cohort that joined in Phase 1.

Phase 3 — Marketing (Q4 2026)

Goal: drive awareness and convert free → Stride+.

  • Creator wave: 8–12 SG-based fitness, food and lifestyle creators in a coordinated 3-week burst with custom referral codes.
  • App Store editorial: Pitch Apple SEA + Google Play to be featured in Health/Fitness collections.
  • PR: Tech in Asia, e27, CNA Lifestyle, Daily SG features.
  • Stride+ launch: Hard paywall on advanced Eat filters, Move map, multi-wearable sync, weekly PDF.
  • Paid pilot: S$5k pilot on Meta + TikTok with creator-style native creative.
  • Go/no-go gate: 5,000 MAU with ≥3% Stride+ conversion before scaling spend.

Phase 4 — User & Partner Onboarding (Q1 2027)

Goal: turn the platform into a marketplace by adding supply (partners) and B2B distribution.

  • Provider Portal beta: Lightweight dashboard for restaurants, gyms, supermarkets to upload menus, calorie data, classes, promotions.
  • Anchor partners (3–5): 1 wellness clinic (e.g., Singapore Sports Hub partner / boutique clinic), 1 gym chain (e.g., Anytime / True Fitness / Ground Zero candidate), 1 healthier-eating chain (e.g., SaladStop! / Grain), 1 supermarket (NTUC FairPrice expansion), 1 corporate wellness pilot.
  • Affiliate links: GrabFood & Foodpanda deep-links from Eat cards.
  • Stride for Teams: One corporate pilot (50–200 seats) with simple HR dashboard.
  • Go/no-go gate: ≥1 anchor partner per category live and producing weekly bookings/redemptions.

14.Risks & SWOT

Strengths
  • Singapore-native food database (real, verified, growing)
  • Simplified v2.0 spec — focus is the differentiator
  • Lean build — fast iteration vs. global incumbents
  • Clear partnership wedge (gyms, restaurants, supermarkets)
Weaknesses
  • Solo / small team — capacity is the constraint
  • No brand awareness in SG yet
  • AI photo scan accuracy unproven at scale
  • No marketplace supply at launch
Opportunities
  • HPB & Healthier SG tailwinds; corporate wellness budgets
  • Underserved SG-specific food data niche
  • Regional expansion (MY, HK, AU) once playbook works
  • Creator economy in SG fitness/food still emerging
Threats
  • MyFitnessPal/Yazio could localise SG content
  • Apple/Google tightening health data permissions
  • API cost inflation (Vision, Places) could squeeze margins
  • HPB Healthy 365 adding tracker features

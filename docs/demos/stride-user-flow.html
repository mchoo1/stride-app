Flow 1 — Setting Up Profile & Goals
First-time onboarding through to personalised calorie & macro targets being calculated
Action step
Decision point
Outcome
1
🚀Open Stride & reach Landing screen
User launches the app for the first time. The Landing screen shows the Stride logo, feature highlights (Smart Tracking, Macro Goals, Activity Log, Meal Planning), and a "Get Started" CTA.
Tap "Get Started"
2
🎯Select a Goal
Onboarding Step 1 asks "What's your goal?" The user picks from four options, each with an icon and description.
⬇️ Lose Weight 💪 Build Muscle ⚖️ Maintain Weight 🏃 Improve Fitness
3
🥗Choose Diet Preference
Onboarding Step 2 shows diet style chips. Selection personalises macro ratios and restaurant filter defaults.
Standard High Protein Low Carb Keto Vegan Vegetarian
4
📋Enter Personal Stats
Onboarding Step 3 collects body data needed to calculate BMR and TDEE. All fields are required before proceeding.
Age Sex Height (cm / ft) Current weight (kg / lb) Target weight Activity level
◆ All required fields filled in?
✗ No
Inline validation highlights empty fields. "Next" button stays disabled until complete.
✓ Yes
Tap "Create Profile" → BMR calculated (Mifflin-St Jeor or Katch-McArdle if body fat % provided).
5
🔢Calorie & Macro Targets Calculated
App computes TDEE from BMR × activity multiplier, then applies a deficit (lose) or surplus (gain) to set a daily calorie target. Macro targets (protein, carbs, fat) are split according to the chosen diet preference.
BMR (kcal) TDEE (kcal) Daily target (kcal) Protein (g) Carbs (g) Fat (g) Est. weeks to goal
6
🏠Arrive at Dashboard
User lands on the Home dashboard showing today's calorie ring, macro progress bars, water intake, and a quick-log button. Profile data is saved to localStorage.
7
✏️Edit Profile Later (optional)
User taps Profile tab → "Edit" button → updates any stat → taps "Save". All downstream targets (TDEE, macros) are recalculated immediately.
Profile → Edit Change weight / goal Save → targets refresh
✅   Profile saved. Daily calorie target & macro goals visible on Dashboard and Profile screens.
🍎
Flow 2 — Tracking & Updating Calories
From opening the food log to seeing updated macro totals on the dashboard
Action step
Decision point
Outcome
1
📋Open Food Log
User taps the "Food" icon in the bottom nav bar (or the + button on Dashboard). The Food Log screen opens showing today's entries grouped by meal (Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner, Snack) plus a macro summary bar.
2
➕Tap a Logging Method
Four quick-access buttons appear at the top of the screen. User selects one entry method.
🔍 Search database 📸 AI Photo Scan ✏️ Manual entry ⭐ Favourites
◆ Which method did the user choose?
🔍 Search
Type food name → results from built-in FOODS database → tap item to select serving size.
📸 AI Scan
Upload or take photo → Clarifai identifies food → USDA macros returned → review card shown.
✏️ Manual
Type name + calories + optional macros (P / C / F) → calories auto-verify formula.
3
🍽️Select Meal Slot
User chooses which meal the food belongs to. The current meal tab is pre-selected based on time of day (morning → Breakfast, etc.).
🌅 Breakfast ☀️ Lunch 🌙 Dinner 🍎 Snack
4
⚖️Confirm Serving Size
A quantity stepper or free-text field lets the user adjust the portion (e.g. ×1.5 servings). Macros scale proportionally in real-time.
5
💾Log Food
Tap "Add to Log" (or "Log Food" in the scan result card). Entry is appended to today's food list under the chosen meal tab.
6
📊Macro Summary Bar Updates
Calories consumed, protein, carbs, and fat totals recalculate instantly. The summary bar at the top of the Food Log and the Dashboard ring both reflect the new totals.
Calories consumed Calories remaining Protein g Carbs g Fat g
7
💧Log Water (optional)
User taps the "+ Water" button on the Dashboard to track hydration alongside calories.
8
🔁Repeat for Each Meal
User repeats steps 2–6 for additional foods throughout the day. Cumulative totals accumulate across all meal slots.
✅   All logged food shows in the Food Log. Dashboard ring and macro bars reflect the updated daily totals.
🏃
Flow 3 — Adding Activities
From opening the activity log to having calories burned reflected on the dashboard
Action step
Decision point
Outcome
1
🏋️Open Activity Log
User taps the "Activity" icon (barbell) in the bottom nav. The Activity Log screen opens, showing the Exercise / Steps tabs and today's logged activities.
2
🔎Choose Entry Method
Two tabs are available at the top of the screen.
💪 Exercise — log named workouts 👟 Steps — log step count directly
◆ Exercise or Steps?
💪 Exercise
Browse 8 preset categories (Cardio, Strength, HIIT, Yoga, Swimming, Cycling, Sports, Other) or type to search. Tap an activity to select it.
👟 Steps
Enter step count manually. App estimates calories as steps × 0.04 kcal.
3
⏱️Set Duration
For exercises, user enters duration in minutes. The app multiplies the activity's MET-based calorie rate × duration to estimate calories burned.
Duration (min) Est. kcal burned shown live
4
➕Tap "Add Activity"
User confirms the entry. The activity is saved to today's activity list with name, duration, and calories burned.
5
🔥Activity Appears in Log
The new activity row appears in the Activity Log list with its emoji, name, duration, and calorie burn badge.
6
📊Dashboard Net Calories Update
The Dashboard recalculates net calories (food consumed − activity burned). The "Calories Burned" card and the calorie ring both update immediately.
Calories burned today Net calories remaining Total steps today
✅   Activity logged. Calories burned added to daily totals. Net calorie budget recalculated on Dashboard.
✏️
Flow 4 — Editing Food & Activities Tracker
Updating or deleting already-logged food entries and activity records
Action step
Decision point
Outcome
1
📂Navigate to the Right Log
User goes to either the Food Log (🍎 tab) or Activity Log (🏋️ tab) depending on what they want to edit.
Food Log → food entries by meal Activity Log → exercise / steps list
2
👆Locate & Tap the Entry
User scrolls to find the logged item. Each entry row shows the food/exercise name, macros/calories, and an edit (✏️) icon or swipe-to-reveal delete option.
◆ Edit or Delete?
✏️ Edit
An edit modal opens pre-filled with current values. User changes quantity / serving / duration / macros. Taps "Save" to confirm.
🗑️ Delete
A confirmation prompt appears. User confirms → entry is removed from the list immediately.
3
🔢Adjust Values
For food: change serving size, quantity multiplier, or individual macro values (P / C / F). Calorie total recalculates using the formula cal = P×4 + C×4 + F×9 to stay consistent.
Serving size × Protein (g) Carbs (g) Fat (g) Calories (auto)
4
💾Save Changes
User taps "Save" (or confirms delete). The list re-renders with the updated entry. A brief toast notification confirms the action.
5
🔄Totals Recalculate
All running totals (calories consumed, macro sums, activity calories burned) are recalculated across the entire day's log. Dashboard and macro summary bar update automatically.
6
✅Verify Macro Accuracy (optional)
The app's "Verify Macro" check can flag entries where the stored calorie value doesn't match the P/C/F formula. User can approve or submit a correction via the verification bar.
Tap "Looks Right" → approved Tap "Fix It" → correct macros saved
✅   Entry updated or removed. All daily totals and macro bars refresh instantly across Food Log and Dashboard.
💡
Flow 5 — Getting Ideas from Plan
Using the Meal Plan tab to discover macro-friendly meal options and log them
Action step
Decision point
Outcome
1
💡Open Plan Tab
User taps the "Plan" icon (lightbulb) in the bottom nav bar. The Plan screen loads with a location context bar at the top.
2
📍Location Detection
The app requests the user's location (or uses a last-known location) to show relevant nearby restaurant suggestions. A city and neighbourhood label is displayed in the context bar.
Allow → precise city shown Deny → generic suggestions shown
3
🍽️Select Meal Slot
Four meal tabs across the top (Breakfast / Lunch / Dinner / Snack) allow the user to browse suggestions relevant to each meal time. The budget bar shows remaining calories for that slot.
🌅 Breakfast ☀️ Lunch 🌙 Dinner 🍎 Snack
4
🏪Filter by Store / Restaurant
Horizontal scrollable store chips (Subway, McDonald's, Starbucks, Chipotle, etc.) let users filter suggestions to a preferred chain. "All" shows the full curated list.
All Subway McDonald's Chipotle Starbucks + more
5
📋Browse Macro-Matched Suggestions
Each suggestion card shows the item name, restaurant, a macro-fit badge (✅ On Track / ⚠️ Over / ❌ Exceeds), calorie count, and P/C/F breakdown. A "Shuffle ↻" button regenerates different options.
Fit badge kcal Protein g Carbs g Fat g
◆ Log suggestion or just browse?
Log It
Tap the "Log" button on a suggestion row → item added to the Food Log under the selected meal slot → macro totals update.
Just Browsing
User takes note of the suggestion for later or refreshes for more ideas. No food log entry is created.
6
🔄Refresh or Change Meal Slot
User taps "↻ Refresh" to load a new set of suggestions, or switches to a different meal tab to plan the rest of the day.
7
📊Daily Tips & Summary
Below the suggestions, a tips section offers context-aware nutrition advice based on the user's current daily log (e.g. "You're low on protein today — consider adding a chicken breast").
✅   Meal ideas discovered. Selected suggestions logged to Food Log. Macro totals and remaining budget updated on Dashboard.
🔬
Flow 6 — Logging Personal Food Macro Details
Entering precise macro values for a custom food item — manually or via AI photo scan
Action step
Decision point
Outcome
1
🔬Choose Entry Path
From the Food Log, the user decides whether to enter macros manually or use the AI photo scan to auto-populate them from an image.
✏️ Manual entry modal 📸 AI Food Scan screen
◆ Manual or AI Scan?
✏️ Manual
Open Manual Entry modal. Type food name. Enter protein, carbs, fat (g). Calories auto-fill using P×4 + C×4 + F×9.
📸 AI Scan
Open AI Food Scan screen. Tap drop-zone or camera button. Upload or capture a photo of the food item.
2
📸AI Scan Pipeline (if photo path)
The image is base64-encoded and sent to /api/scan. The serverless function runs it through Clarifai food-item-recognition → takes the top predicted food name → queries USDA FoodData Central for per-100g macros.
Image → base64 Clarifai identifies food USDA macro lookup (P/C/F/kcal) Confidence % shown
3
🃏Review Macro Result Card
A result card displays: food emoji, recognised name, confidence badge, and the four macro values returned by USDA. User can edit any field inline before logging.
🍎 Food name (editable) Confidence % Calories (kcal) Protein (g) Carbs (g) Fat (g)
4
⚙️Adjust Serving Size & Meal Slot
User tweaks the serving multiplier if the portion differs from 100g. Selects the appropriate meal slot (Breakfast / Lunch / Dinner / Snack). All macros scale proportionally.
5
✅Verify Macro Consistency
The app checks whether the stored calorie value matches P×4 + C×4 + F×9. If there's a discrepancy the Verify bar appears, letting the user approve or fix the values.
✓ Looks Right → keep as is ✎ Fix It → recalculate from macros
6
💾Log Food with Macros
User taps "Log Food". The entry — including all four macro values — is saved to the Food Log under the chosen meal slot.
7
📊Macro Totals Update
The daily protein, carbs, fat, and calorie totals are recalculated. Macro progress bars on the Dashboard and Food Log summary bar reflect the addition.
Total protein consumed Total carbs consumed Total fat consumed Calories consumed Remaining budget
8
🔄Scan Again or Add Another (optional)
If scanning, the result area shows a "Scan Another" button. For manual entry, the modal clears for a new item. User can continue logging until all foods are recorded.
✅   Custom food with precise P/C/F macros saved. Daily macro totals updated. Visible on Food Log summary and Dashboard macro bars.

## Not medical advice

Stride is a general fitness and nutrition **tracking and information** tool. It
is **not** a medical device and does **not** provide medical, dietary, or
healthcare advice. The calorie targets, macro recommendations, and nutrition
information in Stride are for **general informational and educational purposes
only**.

Always consult a qualified doctor, dietitian, or other healthcare professional
before starting any diet, exercise, or weight-management programme — especially
if you are pregnant or breastfeeding, under 18, elderly, or have any medical
condition (including diabetes, heart conditions, eating disorders, or food
allergies). Never disregard professional medical advice because of something you
read or calculated in Stride.

## Nutrition data is estimated

Food and nutrition figures in Stride come from a mix of sources — official brand
nutrition pages, Singapore's Health Promotion Board (HPB), community
submissions, and automated estimates. As a result:

- **Values are estimates and may be inaccurate or out of date.** Actual calories,
  macros, and ingredients vary by outlet, portion size, recipe, and preparation.
- Items shown as **"Estimated"** or **"Community"** are *not* verified for the
  specific outlet and may differ significantly from what you are actually eating.
- **Allergen information, where shown, is not guaranteed.** Always confirm
  allergens and ingredients directly with the restaurant or manufacturer. Do not
  rely on Stride if you have a food allergy or intolerance.

## Calorie & macro targets

Targets are calculated using standard formulas (e.g. Mifflin-St Jeor) from the
information you provide. They are **generic estimates**, not personalised medical
or dietary prescriptions, and individual needs vary widely.

## Food photo scanning

The food-photo feature uses automated AI image recognition to estimate what a
meal is and its nutrition. It can misidentify foods and miscalculate values.
Treat its output as a rough estimate to be checked, not a fact.

## If you are unwell

If you are experiencing a medical emergency, call **995** (Singapore emergency
services) or go to the nearest A&E. If you are struggling with disordered eating
or your relationship with food, please reach out to a healthcare professional or
a support service.
